About Me

I'm Richard, a Stanford student studying Economics and Computer Science with a focus on Human-Computer Interaction. I build products that improve existing systems and experiment with new approaches to make technology more user-friendly and efficient.

My work centers on product engineering—translating user needs into engineering-ready specs, optimizing workflows, and iterating on solutions that deliver measurable impact. I've worked on improving tools at organizations like World Wildlife Fund, optimized agent workflows at Simular.AI, and built mobile apps like MyFlix and Nomi that prioritize clean UX and user alignment.

I'm drawn to projects where product thinking meets technical execution—identifying bottlenecks, refining user experiences, and building systems that work better for the people who use them. Whether it's reducing workflow times by 67% or improving onboarding personalization, I focus on outcomes that matter.

If you're working on product improvements or building user-friendly systems, feel free to get in touch. I'm always interested in connecting with people who are improving how things work.
